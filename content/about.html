Hi, I’m Mark Druffel!

I’m a data scientist, I mutate :coffee: + :beer: into code.

Motivation

I have been working in analytics since ~2013. My undergraduate degree was in business and I started my career in financial and IT audit :sleeping: After a few years, I was lucky enough to land a role managing a Business Intelligence team and my journey in analytics began. Like many data scientists, I started without formal training so I spent a lot of time learning. I set up this blog as a tool to reflect on that process and share it with the community. I hope the content will be useful to other newcomers and encourage collaboration. If you have any feedback or want to discuss any of the topics please feel free to reach out!

A bit about me

I live in Portland with my wonderful wife Brittany. Currently, I am working at start-up called DocStation where we’re building a platform to enable pharmacists to provide value-based care. At DocStation, I have the privilege to work with very smart / amazing engineers who have elevated my engineering skills while I continue to become a better analyst / data scientist. Aside from my work at DocStation, I am active in the local R user group and I’m the author of a few small R packages including harvestR.

In my free-time I love to travel, cook, hike, rock-climb, and see all the beauty the Pacific-Northwest has to offer :mount_fuji:
